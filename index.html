IPFS-SHRINKWRAP

back up your npm dependencies on the permanent web.

Who knows when npm will explode?

Do you trust npm to host all your dependencies over the course of decades, centuries, millenia?

ipfs-shrinkwrap uses IPFS to shrinkwrap all of your dependencies, creating a single, permanent IPFS content address to refer to everything in node_modules/.

use it!

From your project directory: 

ipfs-shrinkwrap

This will place an IPFS hash (content address) in your package.json file.

Then, after the npm-pocalypse: 

ipfs-shrinkwrap install

To install all your dependencies from IPFS. The hash will point to the contents of your node_modules/ directory, at the time the shrinkwrap was done.
